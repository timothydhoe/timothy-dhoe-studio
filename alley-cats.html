Alley Cats

The Brief

Today, the burger joint market is at saturation point. In every city, there are at least four restaurants that prepare the "best burgers in town". Though originally, Alley Cats did not want to break with their strong american vibes using objects like the american flag, horse saddles and other clichés that surpass your wildest nightmares.

We convinced them that it is better to stand out and to stay true to their brand story and values. After all, what is more American than an alley? Well, a bloody delicious burger maybe.

The Opportunity

The alley offers more of its secrets the more you walk through it and you get familiar with its environment. It's also the first place in the city where it gets dark when the sun goes down. The alley is often avoided but for those who dare, it offers opportunity, it has stories to tell and, more than not, it offers you a shortcut to your destination. They aren’t places to be self-conscious or to be taken seriously. The Alley Cat brand needed to reflect this sense of mystery in a fun approach. We noticed that each burger had its particular shape and height. It inspired us to design different icons and labels as a subtle nod to the changing of the ingredients in the burgers and the bun used that determines its shape.

The stories we created are accompanied with a modified colour palette that originated from the American flag. The playful type can be used in different shapes, reminiscent of long shadows that are cast in alley ways. We also designed an alley cat mascot, a representative of the “great unknown that all the secret alleys have to share”, to watch over the restaurant.

The Alley Cats team didn’t have much use for elaborate branding across multiple applications, but where it was required, it needed to stand out and immediately communicate their mysterious and playful nature.
